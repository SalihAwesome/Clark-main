# Product

## Register

product

## Users

General audience — people who have web tasks they want done but don't want to do them manually. They could be researchers, students, professionals, or curious users. They are tech-savvy enough to chat with an agent but not necessarily engineering-focused. Their primary context: they have a goal ("find flights", "fill a form", "research a topic") and Clark is the assistant that does it for them.

## Product Purpose

Clark is a general-purpose autonomous web agent. It takes a goal in natural language, opens a real browser, and works through it step by step — navigating pages, filling forms, searching the web, and reporting back. It exists to make the web do work for you: research, data extraction, form filling, document generation — all through conversation. The product succeeds when users feel like they have a capable, trustworthy assistant that handles the tedious parts of the web.

## Brand Personality

**Playful · Creative · Approachable**

Warm, friendly, never corporate. Clark feels like a capable but slightly quirky assistant — confident enough to drive a real browser, charming enough that you enjoy watching it work. The brand personality sits in the space between "helpful tool" and "delightful companion": competent but not cold, fun but not frivolous, smart but not smug.

Tone keywords: warm, inviting, clear, confident, lightly playful, human-first.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS dark dashboards** — not another admin panel with nav bars and data tables. Clark is an agent, not a dashboard.
- **The current Midnight Tuxedo theme** — the rebrand should feel intentionally different: not cold navy + gold, not terminal-adjacent.
- **Generic AI chatbot aesthetics** — no purple/teal gradients, no robotic glassmorphism, no "AI assistant" clichés.
- **Corporate / Enterprise tools** — no IBM-blue, no gray-flannel seriousness. This is for people, not org charts.
- **Developer / terminal aesthetics** — Clark is for a general audience. No monospace-everything, no green-on-black, no "hacker tool" vibes.

## Design Principles

1. **Playful competence** — It should feel fun AND capable. Delightful details that never undermine trust. The interface smiles without trying too hard.

2. **Warm approachability** — Invites interaction rather than commanding it. Warmth in color, tone, and texture. A tool you *want* to talk to.

3. **Trust through transparency** — Shows what it's doing, when, and why. Every action is visible. When it pauses for you, it's clear what it needs and why.

4. **General-audience first** — Not a developer tool wearing a friendly skin. The interactions, language, and visual system speak to anyone. No jargon, no assumed expertise.

5. **Hand-crafted intentionality** — Nothing feels templated. Every detail — spacing, animation, micro-copy — carries intent. The design has personality without being noisy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text)
- Color is never the sole differentiator
- Reduced motion respected: all animations degrade gracefully
- Clear, plain-English UI copy — no technical jargon
- General audience means accommodating varying technical comfort levels
